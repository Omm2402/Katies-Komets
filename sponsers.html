The Katie Kirlin Fund

Joe and Roseann Kirlin founded Katies Komets and The Katie Kirlin fund in memory of their daughter, Katie Kirlin. Katie Passed away at the age of 12 from cancer. Even though she lived such a short life she accomplished so much. She competed in the National wheelchair Games in Johnson CIty, Tennesse, With 250 other children from around the country. She won a gold metal in the 100-meter race for her injury and age group. She won another metel in the 25 meter swimming competition, Setting a new national record.

Joe and Roseann have helped physically challenged athletes and their families by providing financial support, that fosters participation in wheelchair sports for children in Philadelphia and surrounding areas.

The Carousel House

The Carousel House provides Katies Komets with a practice facility. Also, the Carousel House hosts the annual Katie Kirlin Wheelchair Basketball Tournament, along with many other adaptive sport programs. The Carousel House has been a place for Katies Komets to practice and compete since The very beginning
